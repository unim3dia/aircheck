# Product research notes

## The broadcaster and the year

Howard Stern’s professional radio career began in 1975. His show grew through a 20-year WXRK run, national syndication, and a peak audience reported at roughly 20 million before the move to Sirius. The first Sirius broadcast aired January 9, 2006; George Takei announced, and the show used the “Revelations Game” as a major first-day event.

- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howard_Stern
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Howard_Stern_Show

The source recordings confirm that the 2006 programs are genuinely long-form: the supplied collection contains 179 MP3s, totals 826.5 hours, and averages about 4.6 hours per file. January 9 runs 5:11:46. That length drove resume-first playback, persistent progress, timecode everywhere, and topic navigation rather than a conventional podcast episode list.

## How the official archive organizes a show

The official site’s 2006 month pages group material by broadcast date, then break each date into editorial entries such as **Show Rundown**, **Stern Show Guest**, **Wrap-Up Show**, and named story headlines. A May 16 page exposes a compact rundown—“Beth is Ready to Come Home,” “From WNBC to Sirius,” “Back Off the Cheese,” and “Richard-In-A-Box”—while its story page expands one item into a headline, deck, image, and article.

- https://www.howardstern.com/show/08/2006/
- https://www.howardstern.com/show/2006/05/16/from-wnbc-to-sirius-rundowngallerymodel-12989/

Airhcheck translates that editorial grammar into playable cards. Each story adds the missing archival affordance: an exact audio jump time and matching transcript passage.

## Source boundary

The configured source is:

- https://archive.org/details/howard-stern-24k-complete-2006

Its Internet Archive metadata currently contains no `licenseurl` or `rights` field. The app therefore says “rights status not asserted,” streams from a configurable source, and does not describe the material as public domain. Internet availability alone is not treated as a license.
